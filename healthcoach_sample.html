Physical activity is one of the most powerful tools for managing overall health, particularly when it comes to blood sugar regulation and heart health. When we move our muscles, they use glucose (sugar) for energy, which helps improve insulin sensitivity. This means your body can process sugar more effectively, both during and after your workout. Here are some practical, evidence-based tips to help you stay active: ###
1. Start with "Movement Snacks" You don't need to spend an hour at the gym to see benefits. Small bursts of activity throughout the day—often called "movement snacks"—can be very effective. The Post-Meal Stroll: A 10-to-15-minute walk after meals can significantly help lower post-meal blood sugar spikes. The 30-Minute Rule: If you sit for long periods, try to stand up and move for 2 minutes every 30 minutes. ### 2. Aim for a "Balanced Diet" of Exercise The most effective routine usually includes two types of movement: Aerobic Exercise: Activities like brisk walking, cycling, or swimming get your heart rate up. Aim for about 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week. Resistance Training: Using weights, resistance bands, or your own body weight (like squats or wall push-ups) builds muscle. Muscle tissue burns more glucose than fat tissue, even when you are at rest. Try to do this 2–3 times a week. ### 3. Find Your "Hook" The best exercise is the one you will actually do. If you hate running, don't run! Socialize: Join a walking group or take a dance class.
